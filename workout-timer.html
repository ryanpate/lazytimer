Tabata Timer - The 4-Minute Miracle

The Tabata protocol is a specific HIIT method: 20 seconds of maximum effort followed by 10 seconds rest, repeated 8 times for exactly 4 minutes. Research shows Tabata improves both aerobic and anaerobic fitness more effectively than longer moderate workouts. Our Tabata timer preset is ready to go.

Popular Workout Timer Intervals

  • Tabata: 20 seconds work / 10 seconds rest (4 minutes total)
  • Standard HIIT: 30 seconds work / 30 seconds rest
  • Beginner HIIT: 20 seconds work / 40 seconds rest
  • Advanced HIIT: 45 seconds work / 15 seconds rest
  • Strength Rest: 60-180 seconds between heavy sets
  • Circuit Training: 45 seconds per exercise, 15 seconds transition

Tips for Effective Interval Training

For best results, warm up for 5 minutes before starting intervals. During work periods, push to 80-95% of your maximum effort. During rest, keep moving lightly rather than stopping completely. Start with shorter work intervals and gradually increase intensity as your fitness improves. Track your progress by noting how many rounds you complete. After your workout, use breathing exercises to lower your heart rate and aid recovery. Elite athletes also use strategic power naps of 20-30 minutes post-workout to boost muscle recovery and performance gains.

EMOM Timer - Every Minute on the Minute

EMOM workouts (Every Minute on the Minute) are a CrossFit staple. At the start of each minute, you perform a set number of reps, then rest for the remainder of that minute. When the next minute begins, you go again. Our EMOM timer makes tracking simple—just set it for your total workout time (typically 10-20 minutes) and start each round when the minute changes. EMOMs are excellent for building work capacity and maintaining consistent effort across many rounds.

AMRAP Timer - As Many Rounds As Possible

AMRAP workouts challenge you to complete as many rounds of a circuit as possible within a set time. Common AMRAP durations are 10, 15, or 20 minutes. Our AMRAP timer counts down while you push through rounds of exercises. The goal is to beat your previous round count each time you repeat the workout. AMRAPs are highly effective for conditioning because they encourage maximum effort throughout the entire duration.

Gym Timer for Strength Training

Consistent rest periods are crucial for strength gains. Our gym timer helps you time rest between sets precisely. For maximum strength, rest 3-5 minutes between heavy compound lifts. For hypertrophy (muscle building), keep rest at 60-90 seconds. For muscular endurance, limit rest to 30-45 seconds. Using a timer prevents the common mistake of resting too long (which cools down muscles) or too short (which compromises the next set's performance).

Workout Timer FAQ

What is a HIIT timer?
A HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) timer helps you alternate between intense exercise bursts and rest periods. Typical HIIT workouts use 30-60 second work intervals followed by 15-30 second rest periods. This training method maximizes calorie burn and improves cardiovascular fitness in less time than traditional cardio.
How long should a Tabata workout be?
A traditional Tabata workout is exactly 4 minutes long: 8 rounds of 20 seconds high-intensity exercise followed by 10 seconds rest. This protocol was developed by Japanese scientist Dr. Izumi Tabata and has been proven to improve both aerobic and anaerobic fitness significantly.
What's the best interval for HIIT beginners?
Beginners should start with a 1:2 or 1:3 work-to-rest ratio. For example, 20 seconds of exercise followed by 40-60 seconds of rest. As your fitness improves, gradually decrease rest time until you reach a 1:1 ratio (e.g., 30 seconds work, 30 seconds rest).
How long should I rest between strength training sets?
Rest periods depend on your goals. For strength and power: 2-5 minutes between heavy sets. For muscle growth (hypertrophy): 60-90 seconds. For muscular endurance: 30-60 seconds. Consistent rest timing ensures progressive overload and optimal training adaptation.
Can I use this timer for CrossFit workouts?
Absolutely! Our workout timer is perfect for CrossFit WODs. Use it for AMRAP (As Many Rounds As Possible) workouts, EMOM (Every Minute on the Minute) sessions, or timed rounds. The large display is easy to see during intense workouts.
What is an EMOM workout?
EMOM stands for "Every Minute on the Minute." You start a set of exercises at the beginning of each minute, then rest for the remaining time until the next minute begins. For example: 10 burpees EMOM for 10 minutes. If burpees take 40 seconds, you get 20 seconds rest. EMOMs build work capacity and are popular in CrossFit.
What is an AMRAP workout?
AMRAP means "As Many Rounds As Possible." You perform a circuit of exercises continuously for a set time (usually 10-20 minutes), counting how many complete rounds you finish. AMRAPs are excellent for conditioning because they encourage maximum effort throughout. Track your round count to measure improvement.
How long are boxing rounds?
Professional boxing uses 3-minute rounds with 1-minute rest between rounds. Amateur boxing uses 2-minute rounds. MMA fights use 5-minute rounds. Our boxing timer can be set to any duration for heavy bag work, shadowboxing, pad training, or sparring sessions.
What's the best HIIT workout for beginners?
Beginners should start with a 1:2 or 1:3 work-to-rest ratio—20 seconds of exercise followed by 40-60 seconds of rest. A good starter workout: 20 seconds work, 40 seconds rest, repeat for 10-15 minutes. As fitness improves, gradually increase work time and decrease rest until you reach a 1:1 ratio.
Is interval training better than steady cardio?
For time efficiency, yes. Research shows HIIT burns more calories in less time and continues burning fat for hours after your workout (the "afterburn effect"). HIIT also improves both aerobic and anaerobic fitness simultaneously. However, steady cardio has its place for recovery days and building base endurance.
