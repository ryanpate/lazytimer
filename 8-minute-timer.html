8 Minute Abs Workout Timer

The 8 minute abs workout became famous through fitness videos in the 1990s and remains popular today. It's long enough to work your core thoroughly but short enough to do every day. This format is ideal for beginners building core strength and experienced athletes adding ab work to their routine.

Classic 8-Minute Abs Routine

Perform each exercise for 45 seconds with 15 seconds rest (or continuous for advanced):

  1. Basic crunches - Upper abs, keep lower back pressed down
  2. Reverse crunches - Lower abs, lift hips toward ceiling
  3. Bicycle crunches - Obliques, elbow to opposite knee
  4. Plank hold - Full core stabilization
  5. Side plank (right) - Right oblique engagement
  6. Side plank (left) - Left oblique engagement
  7. Leg raises - Lower abs, keep lower back flat
  8. Mountain climbers - Core and cardio finish

8 Minute HIIT Workout Timer

An 8 minute HIIT session delivers maximum results in minimal time. Research shows that 8 minutes of high-intensity interval training can improve cardiovascular fitness as effectively as longer moderate-intensity workouts.

Sample 8-Minute HIIT Routine

30 seconds work / 30 seconds rest (8 exercises × 1 minute = 8 minutes):

  • Jumping jacks - Cardio warmup to elevate heart rate
  • Burpees - Full body conditioning
  • High knees - Running cardio endurance
  • Mountain climbers - Core and cardio combination
  • Jump squats - Explosive lower body power
  • Push-ups - Upper body strength
  • Plank jacks - Core stability with cardio
  • Speed skaters - Lateral agility finish

8 Minute Cold Exposure Timer

An 8 minute cold exposure timer helps you safely practice cold therapy for health benefits:

Cold Shower Protocol

  • Gradual approach: Start warm, then switch to cold for the final 2-3 minutes
  • Focus areas: Let cold water hit your chest, back, and face for maximum benefit
  • Breathing technique: Controlled exhales help manage the cold shock response
  • Progressive training: Increase cold duration by 30 seconds weekly

Ice Bath Timing

  • Beginner level: 2-3 minutes at 59°F (15°C) is a good starting point
  • Intermediate: 5-8 minutes provides significant benefits
  • Safety first: Never exceed 15 minutes; have a warm towel ready
  • Post-workout: Cold immersion reduces inflammation and muscle soreness

How do I set an 8 minute timer?

What is HIIT training?
HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) alternates between intense exercise bursts and rest periods. An 8-minute HIIT session typically includes 8-16 intervals with 20-30 seconds of work and 10-15 seconds of rest. This timer is perfect for tracking your HIIT workout.
How many seconds is 8 minutes?
8 minutes equals 480 seconds. Our timer displays the countdown in minutes:seconds format (8:00 down to 0:00) and shows visual progress on the analog stopwatch.
